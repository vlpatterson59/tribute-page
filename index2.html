Terry Alan Kath

1946 - 1978

The World's Most Underrated Guitarist

Moments in the life of Terry Kath

  • 1946 - Born in Chicago, Illinois to Raymond Elmer Kath and Evelyn Meline Haugen Kath.
  • 1963 - Joins his first semi-professional band, The Mystics.
  • 1965 - Joins Jimmy Rice and the Gentlemen.
  • 1966 - Joins The Missing Links, a cover band, with fellow musicians Walter "Walt" Parazaider and Daniel "Danny" Seraphine, from Jimmy Rice and the Gentlemen.
  • 1967 - The Big Thing is formed with Kath, Parazaider and Seraphine, and recruits James "Jimmy" Pankow, Lee Loughnane and Robert "Bobby" Lamm. Peter "Pete" Cetera is later recruited to play bass and round out the vocals provided by Kath and Lamm.
  • 1968 - Producer James William Guercio moves the band to Los Angeles, California where they sign with Columbia Records and change their name to Chicago Transit Authority.
  • 1969 - Chicago Transit Authority releases its first album, a self-titled double issue.
  • 1972 - Invests in Pignose Industries.
  • 1973 - Acquires the Fender® Telecaster® which will eventually become his iconic guitar.
  • 1974 - Marries Camelia Emily Ortiz.
  • 1978 - Terry Kath dies from an accidental self-inflicted gunshot wound to the head.
